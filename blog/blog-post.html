  • Payment History: The most crucial factor in determining your credit score is your payment history. It accounts for the largest portion of your score. Consistently making on-time payments for credit cards, loans, and other debts helps maintain a high credit score. Late payments, defaults, or accounts in collections can significantly lower your score.
  • Credit Utilization Ratio: This factor is the percentage of your credit limit that you're currently using. It's recommended to keep your credit utilization below 30% for each credit card and across all credit cards combined. A lower credit utilization ratio positively affects your credit score.
  • Length of Credit History: The length of time you've had credit accounts is important. A longer credit history is generally viewed more favorably, as it provides a more extended track record of managing credit responsibly.
